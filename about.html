About BCSSJ

A cultural home for Bengali families in South Jersey

Founded in 1978, BCSSJ exists to celebrate, preserve, and share Bengali culture through festivals, arts, education, and service.

Our mission

To promote, preserve, and share the rich tapestry of Bengali and Indian culture while fostering a strong sense of community and understanding across South Jersey.

Our vision

To create a space where children grow up proud of their Bengali heritage, families remain deeply connected to tradition, and the timeless values of Bengal enrich the multicultural fabric of America.

What we do

Festivals & Pujos

Durga Pujo, Lakshmi Pujo, Kali Pujo, Saraswati Pujo, Basanta Utsav, and more.

Arts & Performance

Music, dance, theater, literature, and youth participation across generations.

Education & Identity

Programs that help children and families stay connected to language, stories, and cultural roots.

Community & Service

Volunteer-driven efforts that support both local connection and broader charitable impact.
